About

The Sirens of Circe began in the 1990s as a house band at the New York Renaissance Faire known as the Singing Wenches. From there, we evolved into the classier Sirens of Sterling, and beginning in 2021, we set sail and have been traveling the country as the Sirens of Circe.

The Sirens of Circe provide an all-female musical show that is both bawdy and beautiful, often in the same song. From classic a cappella ballads like Both Sides the Tweed and Wild Mountain Thyme, to innuendo-laden upbeats like Cuckoo’s Nest and Caviar, to TikTok favorites like Savage Daughter and My Mother Told Me, no two Sirens sets are the same. We cater our shows to our audiences, everything from family-friendly to 21+.
